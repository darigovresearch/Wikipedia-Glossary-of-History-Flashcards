<dd class=""glossary"">A small, tightly organised, revolutionary <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marxism"" title=""Marxism"">Marxist</a> group in early 20th-century <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russia"" title=""Russia"">Russia</a> which split from the Russian Socialist movement in 1903 and was led by <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vladimir_Lenin"" title=""Vladimir Lenin"">Vladimir Lenin</a>. In November 1917, during the so-called <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/October_Revolution"" title=""October Revolution"">October Revolution</a>, the Bolsheviks (""Majority"") took control of a chaotic Russia, becoming the <i><a href=""#de_facto""><span title=""See entry on this page at § de facto"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">de facto</span></a></i> rulers after <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Civil_War"" title=""Russian Civil War"">the subsequent civil war</a>. However, various types of stone, particularly flint, remained very important long after metal became available. The Bronze Age saw the introduction of cremation of the dead and burials in round barrows. The later (and best-known) phases of construction at Stonehenge also date from this period.</dd>

When the Roman leader <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaius_Julius_Caesar"" title=""Gaius Julius Caesar"">Gaius Julius Caesar</a> was assassinated in 44 BCE, his nephew and successor <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Augustus"" title=""Augustus"">Augustus</a> had himself formally adopted by the dead man and so also adopted the family name Caesar. <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tiberius"" title=""Tiberius"">Tiberius</a> and <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caligula"" title=""Caligula"">Caligula</a> inherited it by adoption as well. Later Roman emperors acquired the name upon their succession or when they were formally adopted as heirs.</dd>

The context in which an <a href=""#artifact""><span title=""See entry on this page at § artifact"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">artifact</span></a> is found provides important evidence for its interpretation.</dd>

<dt class=""glossary"" id=""cretaceous_western_interior_seaway"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Interior_Seaway"" title=""Western Interior Seaway"">Cretaceous Western Interior Seaway</a></dfn></dt>

At its peak in the Middle Cretaceous (~90 Ma), it extended from present-day Utah to the Appalachians and from the Arctic to the Gulf of Mexico.</dd>

History relates to past events as well as the memory, discovery, collection, organization, presentation and interpretation of information about these events.</dd>

<dt class=""glossary"" id=""imperialism"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imperialism"" title=""Imperialism"">imperialism</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary""></dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""interdisciplinary"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interdisciplinary"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Interdisciplinary"">interdisciplinary</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">The study or practice of a subject which applies the methods and approaches of several <a href=""#discipline""><span title=""See entry on this page at § discipline"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">disciplines</span></a>. For instance, while history, literature and archaeology are separate disciplines, they may be combined in an interdisciplinary approach.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""journal"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_journal"" title=""History journal"">journal</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">A scholarly periodical, often focusing on a particular historical theme.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""lacuna"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lacuna_(manuscripts)"" title=""Lacuna (manuscripts)"">lacuna</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">A gap in a manuscript, inscription or text.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""landscape_history"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landscape_history"" title=""Landscape history"">landscape history</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary""></dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""laurasia"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laurasia"" title=""Laurasia"">Laurasia</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">A <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supercontinent"" title=""Supercontinent"">supercontinent</a> that existed from the Jurassic to Early Tertiary after splitting from <a href=""#pangaea""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Pangaea"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Pangaea</span></a>. It was composed of <a href=""#laurentia""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Laurentia"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Laurentia</span></a>, <a href=""#baltica""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Baltica"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Baltica</span></a>, and <a href=""#avalonia""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Avalonia"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Avalonia</span></a> (what is now North America, Scandinavia, Greenland, and Western and Central Europe), and eventually fragmented into Eurasia and North America in the Tertiary with the opening of the North Atlantic Ocean.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""laurentia"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laurentia"" title=""Laurentia"">Laurentia</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">A separate continental plate that existed from the Late Precambrian to Silurian, consisting of the major part of what is now North America, northwest Ireland, Scotland, Greenland, and pieces of Norway and Russia.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""local_history"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_history"" title=""Local history"">local history</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">The study of the history of a small geographical area, of a local community, or of the local incidence of broader national or international trends. If undertaken with a view to casting light on larger historical questions, local history may be regarded as a branch of <a href=""#microhistory""><span title=""See entry on this page at § microhistory"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">microhistory</span></a>.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""&#39;&#39;longue_durée&#39;&#39;"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><i><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Longue_dur%C3%A9e"" title=""Longue durée"">longue durée</a></i></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary""></dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""macrohistory"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macrohistory"" title=""Macrohistory"">macrohistory</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">The study of large, long-term trends in <a href=""#world_history""><span title=""See entry on this page at § world history"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">world history</span></a>, undertaken in order to uncover ultimate patterns that cut across the more specific details of diverse historical cultures.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""manuscript"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manuscript"" title=""Manuscript"">manuscript</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">Any document written by hand, as opposed to one that is printed or reproduced in some other way.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""microhistory"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microhistory"" title=""Microhistory"">microhistory</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">The intensive historical investigation of a small and narrow unit of research (an event, a community or an individual), generally undertaken with a view to casting light on broader historical questions. <a href=""#local_history""><span title=""See entry on this page at § local history"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Local history</span></a> may be considered a branch of microhistory.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""migration"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_migration"" title=""Human migration"">migration</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary""></dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""military_history"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military_history"" title=""Military history"">military history</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">The study of the history of armed conflict and its impact on society. It may range from the study of specific military actions and engagements to the much broader examination of <a href=""#warfare""><span title=""See entry on this page at § warfare"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">warfare</span></a> as a political tool.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""modern_history"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modern_history"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Modern history"">modern history</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary""></dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""mythology"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mythology"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Mythology"">mythology</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">The collected body of <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myth"" title=""Myth"">myths</a> shared by a culture or a group of people, or the academic study of such myths.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""numismatics"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Numismatics"" title=""Numismatics"">numismatics</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">The study of coins.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""natural_history"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_history"" title=""Natural history"">natural history</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">A domain of inquiry involving organisms including animals, fungi and plants in their natural environments which leans more towards observational than experimental methods of study.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""onomastics"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Onomastics"" title=""Onomastics"">onomastics</a></dfn></dt>
<p class=""glossary-hatnote""><span role=""note"" class=""hatnote navigation-not-searchable selfref"">Also <b>onomatology</b>.</span></p>
<dd class=""glossary"">The study of the <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Etymology"" title=""Etymology"">etymology</a>, history, and use of <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proper_name"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Proper name"">proper names</a>.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""original_order"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Original_order"" title=""Original order"">original order</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">A concept in archival theory which proposes that a group of records should be maintained in the same order as they were placed by their creator.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""paleography"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paleography"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Paleography"">paleography</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">The study of old handwriting.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""paleo-tethys_ocean"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paleo-Tethys_Ocean"" title=""Paleo-Tethys Ocean"">Paleo-Tethys Ocean</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">A large ocean that originated between eastern <a href=""#gondwana""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Gondwana"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Gondwana</span></a>, <a href=""#siberia""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Siberia"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Siberia</span></a>, Kazakhstan and <a href=""#baltica""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Baltica"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Baltica</span></a> in the Ordovician and finally closed in the Jurassic. It was replaced by the Tethys Ocean as eastern <a href=""#pangaea""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Pangaea"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Pangaea</span></a> was assembled.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""pangaea"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pangaea"" title=""Pangaea"">Pangaea</a></dfn></dt>
<p class=""glossary-hatnote""><span role=""note"" class=""hatnote navigation-not-searchable selfref"">Also spelled <b>Pangea</b>.</span></p>
<dd class=""glossary"">A <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supercontinent"" title=""Supercontinent"">supercontinent</a> that existed from the end of the Permian to the Jurassic, assembled from large continents like <a href=""#euramerica""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Euramerica"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Euramerica</span></a>, <a href=""#gondwana""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Gondwana"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Gondwana</span></a> and <a href=""#siberia""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Siberia"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Siberia</span></a>, as well as smaller landmasses like the <a href=""#cathaysian_Terranes""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Cathaysian Terranes"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Cathaysian</span></a> and <a href=""#cimmerian_Terranes""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Cimmerian Terranes"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Cimmerian Terranes</span></a>. The name <i>Pangaea</i> is Greek for “all lands”.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""pannotia"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pannotia"" title=""Pannotia"">Pannotia</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">A <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supercontinent"" title=""Supercontinent"">supercontinent</a> that existed in the Late Precambrian and gave rise to the continents of <a href=""#gondwana""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Gondwana"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Gondwana</span></a>, <a href=""#laurentia""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Laurentia"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Laurentia</span></a>, <a href=""#siberia""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Siberia"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Siberia</span></a> and <a href=""#baltica""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Baltica"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Baltica</span></a> in the Cambrian.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""panthalassic_ocean"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Panthalassic_Ocean"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Panthalassic Ocean"">Panthalassic Ocean</a></dfn></dt>
<p class=""glossary-hatnote""><span role=""note"" class=""hatnote navigation-not-searchable selfref"">Also called the <b><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Panthalassa"" title=""Panthalassa"">Panthalassa</a></b>.</span></p>
<dd class=""glossary"">A vast ocean that existed from the Late Precambrian to the Jurassic, circling the globe and connecting to smaller oceans that developed throughout the Phanerozoic.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""past"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Past"" title=""Past"">past</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">The entire set or any subset of events which happened previously in time.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""periodization"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Periodization"" title=""Periodization"">periodization</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary""></dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""philately"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philately"" title=""Philately"">philately</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">The study of postage stamps.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""philology"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philology"" title=""Philology"">philology</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary""></dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""political_history"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_history"" title=""Political history"">political history</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">The study of political events, ideas, movements and leaders of the past.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""prehistory"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prehistory"" title=""Prehistory"">prehistory</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary""></dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""presentism"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presentism_(literary_and_historical_analysis)"" title=""Presentism (literary and historical analysis)"">presentism</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">The application of present-day ideas and perspectives to depictions or interpretations of the past.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""primary_source"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Primary_source"" title=""Primary source"">primary source</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">Material from or directly related to the past. The term usually refers to records and documents created during the period that is being studied, such as diaries, letters, legal documents, accounts, photographs, news reports, and <a href=""#artifact""><span title=""See entry on this page at § artifact"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">artefacts</span></a>. Contrast <i><a href=""#secondary_source""><span title=""See entry on this page at § secondary source"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">secondary source</span></a></i>.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""prosopography"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prosopography"" title=""Prosopography"">prosopography</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">The investigation of a historical group of individuals through a collective study of their lives.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""protohistory"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protohistory"" title=""Protohistory"">protohistory</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary""></dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""provenance"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Provenance"" title=""Provenance"">provenance</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">The chronology of the ownership, custody or location of a historical object, document or group of records.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""pseudohistory"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pseudohistory"" title=""Pseudohistory"">pseudohistory</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary""></dd>

<dd class=""glossary"">A range of activities undertaken by people with some training in the discipline of history, but who are generally working outside of specialized academic settings.</dd>

<dd class=""glossary""></dd>

<dd class=""glossary""></dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""respect_des_fonds"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Respect_des_fonds"" title=""Respect des fonds"">respect des fonds</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">An archival principle which proposes that collections of archival records should be ordered and preserved according to the administration, organization, individual, or entity by which they were created or from which they were received.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""revolution"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Revolution"" title=""Revolution"">revolution</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary""></dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""rodinia"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rodinia"" title=""Rodinia"">Rodinia</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">A <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supercontinent"" title=""Supercontinent"">supercontinent</a> that existed during the Late Precambrian before the supercontinent <a href=""#pannotia""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Pannotia"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Pannotia</span></a>, and the oldest supercontinent for which scientists have a good record. The name <i>Rodinia</i> is Russian for ""homeland"".</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""romanticism"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romanticism"" title=""Romanticism"">Romanticism</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">A cultural and intellectual movement of the late 18th to mid-19th centuries that emphasized emotion and sentiment rather than reason, predominantly among Western European cultures but also in other parts of the world.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""reference_work"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reference_work"" title=""Reference work"">reference work</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">A text, usually in the form of a dictionary or encyclopedia, which contains facts and information but typically not discussions.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""saeculum"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saeculum"" title=""Saeculum"">saeculum</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">A length of time approximately equal to the potential lifetime of a human being or, equivalently, to the time it takes to completely regenerate a human population with new individuals – that is, the duration between the moment at which an event occurs (such as the founding of a city) and the point in time at which every individual who was alive at the first moment has died.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""secondary_source"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secondary_source"" title=""Secondary source"">secondary source</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">Material created by somebody removed from the event being studied; i.e. someone who was contemporaneous with the event but not physically present to witness it, or who was working from a period of time after the event occurred. All historical textbooks, for example, are secondary sources. Contrast <i><a href=""#primary_source""><span title=""See entry on this page at § primary source"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">primary source</span></a></i>.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""seal"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seal_(emblem)"" title=""Seal (emblem)"">seal</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">A device for making an impression, usually in wax, or the impression so formed, historically used to authenticate documents.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""siberia"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siberia_(continent)"" title=""Siberia (continent)"">Siberia</a></dfn></dt>
<p class=""glossary-hatnote""><span role=""note"" class=""hatnote navigation-not-searchable selfref"">Also called <b>Angaraland</b>, <b>Angara</b> or <b>Angarida</b>.</span></p>
<dd class=""glossary"">A separate continental plate that existed from the Latest Precambrian to the Carboniferous, composed of a large part of what is now central Russia, namely the modern region of <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siberia"" title=""Siberia"">Siberia</a>.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""sigillography"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sigillography"" title=""Sigillography"">sigillography</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">The study of <a href=""#seal""><span title=""See entry on this page at § seal"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">seals</span></a>.</dd>
<dt class=""glossary"" id=""social_history"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_history"" title=""Social history"">social history</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class=""glossary"">A branch of history that studies the experiences of ordinary people in the past.</dd>
